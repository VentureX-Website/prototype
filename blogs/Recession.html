Start-ups and the Recession 2023: What can they do to prepare for it?

  • Aditi R Jahagirdar....
  • Jun 25, 2023

RECESSION IN 2023

"The greatest entrepreneurs thrive during times of uncertainty and adversity."

-Richard Branson

The takeaway from this quote is that successful entrepreneurs are able to achieve their goals under challenging and unpredictable circumstances.


In 2023, the recession will have a significant negative impact on the economic expansion of many countries. It poses some unique challenges to test their strategic skills. Several businesses may have the following effects during a recession:

  • Limited Cash Flow : In a recession, consumer spending tends to lower sales for the business. Start-ups may therefore find it challenging to retain their sales and a positive cash flow.
  • Reduced Funding Opportunities : During an economic downturn, investors tend to become more cautious about their investments to avoid the financial risk associated with a recession. Investors' reduced interest in taking risks can make startups harder to get funding.
  • Recruiting and Retaining Talent : The scarcity of jobs is likely to increase the competition for talent. Startup companies struggle to retain their employees because employees tend to move away to more successful jobs that offer higher salaries and more stability.
  • A reduction in consumer spending :As consumers become more prudent, consumer spending decreases during recessions. Customer demand can directly impact startups. Sales and revenue of startups may decline, making it difficult to sustain growth and cover operational costs.
  • Instability and volatility in the market : Markets become unpredictable and uncertain when the economy isn't doing well. New businesses have difficulty making plans and smart decisions because of this. A startup's ability to adapt and change with changes in market conditions, customer preferences, and industry trends may not be predictable.
  • In spite of these disadvantages, startups that are resilient, adaptable, and innovative can still find success and growth during recessions. As long as startups manage their finances, stay agile, focus on customer needs, and explore alternative strategies, they can survive and thrive in the long run. This can be achieved by the following strategies:
  • Establish relationships with investors : Regardless of whether there is no financing immediately, new businesses ought to continue to assemble associations with financial backers. They will remain for potential investments in the future if they demonstrate progress and maintain open communication.
  • Vary in funding sources : investigate alternative funding options such as government grants, assistance from the financial institution (bank) crowdfunding platforms, or strategic partnerships.
  • Market investigation and client division : During the downfall, consumer spending patterns change, and new businesses need to adjust their strategies accordingly. Entrepreneurs can locate new target markets or change their product line by analyzing the entire market and understanding changing customer requirements. It can help new businesses to contact the right audience with the appropriate arrangements by grouping their client base and fitting promotional messages.
  • Customer relationship management and retention : Maintaining customer loyalty becomes increasingly crucial during a recession.Entrepreneurs should concentrate on providing exceptional client experiences, providing value-added services, and maintaining suitable communication channels.

Airbnb is a prime example of a startup that flourished in a downturn. The business was established in 2008, When it first launched, it received very little attention, like many start-ups. However, during the global financial crisis, when the economy was severely depressed, they observed a trend in the images of their commercials. Since the owners just used iPhones to take photos, they weren't particularly attractive.

Because consumers weren't sure what they would be paying for, they didn't receive many reservations. As a result, representatives from Airbnb came to New York to meet the hosts and assist them in taking professional photos. Within a week, this non-technical, non-scalable solution had doubled its revenue.


In order to better serve the demands of platform users, Airbnb keeps testing new concepts. Although Airbnb's performance during a recession is instructive, the road and success criteria for each firm may vary. Finding market possibilities, providing clients with value, and having a strong company strategy that can change with the economy are the keys.

Startups can position themselves for strength and success with the right preparation and strategic approach, despite the fact that the recession of 2023 may present obstacles. Startups can navigate the downturn and emerge stronger from it by focusing on financial preparedness, customer-centric strategies, agility, and collaboration.


Remember that flexibility, development, and a client-centered outlook are urgent in getting ready for and enduring the effect of the downturn!!
